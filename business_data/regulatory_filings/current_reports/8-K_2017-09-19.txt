<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL"><I>Annual shareholders meeting set for Nov. 29, 2017 </I></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL"><B>REDMOND, Wash. &#151;
Sept. 19, 2017 &#151;</B> Microsoft Corp. on Tuesday announced that its board of directors declared a quarterly dividend of $0.42 per share, reflecting a 3 cent or 7.6&nbsp;percent increase over the previous quarter&#146;s dividend. The dividend is
payable Dec. 14, 2017, to shareholders of record on Nov. 16, 2017. The <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ex-dividend</FONT> date will be Nov. 15, 2017. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">The company
also announced the date for the 2017 annual shareholders meeting, to be held Nov. 29, 2017. Shareholders at the close of business on Sept. 29, 2017, the record date, will be entitled to vote at the annual shareholders meeting. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">In addition, the company announced the appointment of Hugh Johnston, vice chairman and chief financial officer of PepsiCo, to its board of directors and to the
board&#146;s audit committee, effective immediately. Johnston brings valuable experience through his nearly <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">30-year</FONT> history at PepsiCo, the global food and beverage company whose product portfolio includes 22
brands that generate more than $1&nbsp;billion each in estimated annual retail sales.<B> </B> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Johnston, 56, has served as the chief financial officer since 2010
and became vice chairman in 2015. In his career at PepsiCo, he has served as executive vice president of global operations, president of Pepsi-Cola North America, senior vice president of transformation, and senior vice president of mergers and
acquisitions. Johnston has previously served as a director and audit committee chair for Twitter Inc. and AOL Inc. He currently serves as a director for the Peterson Institute for International Economics, on the University of Chicago&#146;s Booth
School CFO Advisory Board, and on Syracuse University&#146;s Whitman School of Management Advisory council. Adding Johnston increases Microsoft&#146;s board to 13 members. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">&#147;Hugh is an accomplished financial and business leader who brings a depth of experience from his successful career at PepsiCo where he has oversight of finance,
IT, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">e-commerce</FONT> and the company&#146;s digital transformation,&#148; said John W. Thompson, independent Microsoft board chairman. &#147;He is a great addition to Microsoft&#146;s board of directors, and we look
forward to working with him.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Microsoft also announced that G. Mason Morfit, president and chief investment officer of ValueAct Capital, will not seek <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-election</FONT> to the board. His current term expires at the annual shareholders meeting in November. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">&#147;Mason has been a
valuable adviser at an important time in Microsoft&#146;s transformation, and we are grateful for his contributions,&#148; Thompson said. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Other board members
include John W. Thompson, Microsoft independent chairman; William H. Gates, Microsoft founder and technology advisor; Reid Hoffman, partner at Greylock Partners; Teri L. List-Stoll, executive vice president and chief financial officer of Gap Inc.;
Satya Nadella, chief executive officer of Microsoft; Charles H. Noski, former vice chairman of Bank of </P>
